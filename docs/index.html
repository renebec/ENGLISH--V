In context

1. Read the following text about tourism industry.

What is the tourism industry?
So, what is the tourism industry? Essentially, it refers to all activity related to the short-term movement of people to locations away from where they usually reside.
The tourist
In simple terms, a tourist is a person travelling to another location, away from their usual social environment, for business, pleasure or social reasons. By most accepted definitions, a person is considered a tourist if he/she stays at that location for longer than 24 hours, but no longer than one year.
What are the benefits of the tourism industry?
Tourism has many benefits, including economic benefits for countries attracting a lot of visitors. It also provides many works for people working in the transport and hospitality industry.

What are the sectors in the tourist industry?
1. Transportation: this sector is concerned with helping tourists to get where they need to go. Included in this sector are services related to the airline industry, car rental, water transport, taxis, buses and trains.
2. Acommodation: this acommodation sector is central to the travel and hospitality industry because people travelling to different areas require somewhere to stay, eat, rest and sleep. It includes the hotel industry, camping, hostels, bed & breakfast, and cruises.
3. Food and beverage:this sector provide tourists with essential refreshments during their travel experience, including during travel, when spending time in their chosen acommodation, and when they are out and about exploring the location they have travelled to.
4. Entertainment: some tourists travel to new locations in the pursuit of entertainment. This sector includes casinos, shopping, tours and all kinds of local attractions and shows.
5. Connected industries: finally, there are also several industries that are either directly or indirectly connected to the tourism industry. Examples of these are financial services, travel agencies, tour operators, online travel agencies, tourism organizations, marketing and sales, and technology.

💬 To speak and write correctly

In the first part of this partial, we are going to study Modal Verbs. You already know some of them, for example: can, will, would. We use modal verbs in English to express things like ability, permission, possibility, obligation, etc. They only have one form. They do not take -s in the simple present and they do not have a past simple or past participle form. Here we'll work with Must, Have to, Should, Shouldn't and May. They are used to express obligations, advice, probability and concern.

MUST
•You must carry your pasport everywhere you go
•You must learn some useful phrases in the language of the country you visit.		MUST is used to express STRONG NECESSITY
•You must follow the rules inside the swimming pool.
•You must wait for your turn to use the treadmill at the gym.		MUST is used to express RULES and REGULATIONS
MUST + verb (simple form)
•You must help the customers whenever you can.
•She must wear her ID inside the congress premises.
NEGATIVE
•You must not feed the the fish in the aquarium.
•You mustn't go into the ocean if there are strong winds.

💬 To speak and write correctly

HAVE TO
•You have to be careful with your key card.
•All staff members have to follow safety rules.
•Miguel has to organized the security staff.		HAVE TO is used to express STRONG NECESSITY
HAVE/HAS TO + verb (simple form)
•I have to place my valuables in the safe deposit box.
•You have to tell the front desk clerk if you are carrying a laptop, jewelry or any other valuable things.
•He/She has to be careful at night.
•We have to read the safety recommendations at the hotel.
NEGATIVEIn the negative it means that something is NOT NECESSARY
•You don't have to go to the restaurant. You may ask for room service.
•He doesn't have to drive. Everything is at walking distance.		We use the auxilliary DO to form the negative:
DON'T HAVE TO
DOESN'T HAVE TO
INTERROGATIVE
•Do we have to attend all the conferences?
•No, you don't. You just have to attend two of them.
•Does she have to register all her valuables?
•Yes, she does. She has to register her laptop, camera and jewelry.
•We use the auxilliary DO to form the interrogative.
•DO + SUBJECT + HAVE TO
•DOES + SUBJECT (THIRD PERSON SINGULAR) + HAVE TO
